Activity 1: Team Identity Forge

A fun icebreaker to provide an immediate, creative win with AI and help bond new teams.

Psychology Purpose

This activity accelerates the "Forming" stage of group development. By creating a shared, non-threatening goal, it builds psychological safety and cohesion, allowing teams to bypass initial awkwardness and form a collective identity much faster.

Pro Tips

Use this for any new team or class to break the ice. You can iterate on the logo prompt (e.g., "add a vintage style," "use a vibrant color palette") to also teach the valuable skill of prompt refinement and creative iteration with AI.

Step 1: Brainstorm a Team Name

Use the following prompt to generate a list of potential team names.

Brainstorm 10 team names for a psychology workshop group. The names should combine a well-known psychological theory or theorist (like Maslow, Skinner, Jung) with a mythical creature (like a Griffin, Phoenix, or Hydra).

Step 2: Create a Team Logo

Once your team has chosen a name, use this prompt to create a unique logo.

Create a minimalist, modern logo for a team called "[Chosen Name]". The logo should visually represent [a simple description of the name, e.g., 'a phoenix rising from a pyramid']. Use a clean, two-color palette.

Activity 2: The Content Transformer

Learn how a single block of academic text can be transformed into multiple, distinct learning tools.

Psychology Purpose

This directly applies Cognitive Load Theory. Transforming dense text into a quiz (active recall) or an infographic (dual coding) reduces unnecessary mental strain and promotes deeper, more effective learning and long-term memory encoding.

Pro Tips

Use this for your most challenging subjects. You can even ask the AI to "create a 5-question quiz on the most critical concepts" or "design an infographic for a 10-year-old" to force simplification and clarify your own understanding.

Activity 3: India's Empathy Map

Quickly build a nuanced understanding of diverse regional attitudes and resources for mental health in India.

Psychology Purpose

This is a practical exercise in developing cultural humility. It's not about becoming an expert in another culture, but about fostering a respectful curiosity. This AI-driven approach is a tool for practicing that curiosity quickly and effectively, which is essential for building a strong therapeutic alliance.

Pro Tips

Use this as a 5-minute prep before a first client meeting if you know their background. A quick search provides invaluable context. To deepen your understanding, try a follow-up prompt like, "What are common communication styles in [Region]?"

Step 1: Explore Cultural Views

Use this prompt to find and summarize a video about mental health perspectives in your assigned region.

@YouTube: Find a short YouTube video (under 10 minutes) that explains the cultural views or challenges related to mental health in [State/Region, India]. Summarize the key takeaways from the video.

Step 2: Find Local Resources

Now, use this prompt to identify different types of mental health support centers in a major city in that region.

@Google Maps: Find three different types of mental health support centers in [Major City, State]. Try to find a psychiatric hospital, a private counseling center, and an NGO or community-based center. List their names and general locations, and then open a map showing their locations.

Activity 7: Game Concept to Playable Prototype

Turn a simple text description of a game into a live, playable prototype.

Psychology Purpose

This activity applies principles of behaviorism and gamification. Game mechanics like points and rules are forms of operant conditioning (reinforcement) that can make learning therapeutic concepts more engaging and less intimidating, especially for younger audiences.

Pro Tips

Focus on the "core loop" of your game: the one action the player repeats (e.g., match a bias to a definition -> get a point). A simple core loop makes for the best prototype. You can also ask the AI to "add a scoring system" or "a 60-second timer" to test different mechanics.
